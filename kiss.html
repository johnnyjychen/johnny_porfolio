UX Research - KISS

Project Information

Overview: In this project, our group assisted our clients in conducting a range of UX research for the KISS app, which enabled users to log their sexual activities and share their sexual health status with partners. Our work included user interviews, surveys, comparative evaluations, heuristic evaluations, and user testing in a course.

Project Type: Group Project

Duration: 2/2024 - 4/2024

Skills: UX Research, User Interviews, Surveys, Comparative Evaluations, Heuristic Evaluations, User Testing, Presentation

User Interviews

Our client was the KISS app, featuring QR code registrations and anonymous STI notifications. We interviewed five diverse, sexually active individuals to enhance the app's usability and understand user perspectives on sexual health. The key recommendations included adding a feature for sharing verified STI results, enhancing data protection, providing customizable privacy settings, and enabling direct submission of verified results. Our research aimed to explore attitudes towards sexual activities outside one's usual circle and gather feedback on the app's main features.

Surveys

We synthesized a pilot survey and participant feedback designed to gauge potential KISS APP users' attitudes towards STI prevention, perspectives on STI testing, and receptiveness to an anonymous notification feature. The pilot testing process ensured survey validity, helped identify logical discrepancies, and accurately discerned user needs and insights sought by our client. These results informed the app's development and enhancements, aligning with user expectations and contributing to STI prevention initiatives.

Comparative Evaluations

We conducted comparative evaluations of the KISS app and other sexual wellness applications using SWOT analysis and feature comparison methods. We evaluated competitors including Sexual Activity, Kama, Nice, STD Triage, and SLog. Key findings emphasized privacy, user-friendly design, and the importance of educational content, notably the privacy-enhancing aspect of non-required sign-ups. Recommendations included minimizing sign-up processes, refining navigation, enhancing educational content, and adding features like clinic finding and online diagnosis to improve user experience and market positioning of the KISS app.

Heuristic Evaluations

In this research, we conducted several heuristic analyses of the KISS application. The KISS app was designed to enhance sexual wellness and prevent the spread of STIs through features like anonymous partner notifications. This analysis focused on evaluating the app's usability, especially the “Main Page,” "New Kiss," and "New Screening" pages, to identify usability issues and recommend improvements.

User Testing

To enhance feedback on the KISS app's features, we conducted user testing on more users, observing behaviors and gathering feedback. We developed a user testing protocol with a pre-questionnaire, background questions, five tasks, and a post-questionnaire. After a team member piloted this script and identified potential improvements, we added a three-minute limit for tasks if interviewees struggled. With five team members, each conducted a test with one interviewee, recording the sessions for analysis.
